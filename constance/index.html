After studying cryptography at Telecom ParisTech and machine learning at KTH (Stockholm), I started my career at Morpho Safran (now IDEMIA) as a cryptography researcher in biometrics.
For four years, I worked on cooperative projects on how to secure biometric authentication. This research has led to the publication of several articles in international conferences and the filing of some patents.

Then, I joined BNP Paribas as an IT quantitative researcher. For three years, I worked on the design and improvement of services to traders.

Since 2020, I am a privacy research scientist at Owkin where my research focuses on privacy preserving machine learning in healthcare.
