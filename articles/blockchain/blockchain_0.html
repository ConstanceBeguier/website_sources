Introduction

The first blockchain has been introduced by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2008 in the paper [Nak08]. The blockchain has been created to build a cryptocurrency called bitcoin without third trusted party. This blockchain is the ledger of this cryptocurrency and stores all transactions from the genesis transaction. A blockchain has the following properties:

  • Openness: everyone can check all the blocks of the blockchain
  • Integrity: the blockchain is copied on different computers which avoids an attacker to hack it
  • Without trusted third party: each client play a role in the management of the database

The blockchain has been created for bitcoin but is now used in various altcoins (other cryptocurrencies) and altchains (aim is not currency but distributed consensus).

To make it easier the understanding of the blockchain, we present its functioning in real bitcoin applications with three subjects: a client called Alice who spends bictoins, a seller called Bob who receives bitcoins and a miner called Mickael who checks the validity of the transactions.

Firstly we present the functioning of the blockchain on the client side (in Section Client side: How to receive and send a transaction). Then we explain the structure of the blockchain (ledger) to store the transactions (in Section The blockchain to store all the transactions). Finally we explain how transactions are checked and validated by miners (in Section Miner side: The validation of transactions).
