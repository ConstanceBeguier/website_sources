The functioning of financial markets

Financial market structure and participants

Five kinds of financial markets:
  • exchange/currency market
  • equity/stock market
  • interest-rate market
  • commodity market
  • credit market
Two kinds of market products:
  • Financial securities: direct capital movement (ex: stocks, bonds, ...)
  • Financial derivatives: no direct capital movement(ex: futures, forwards, options, swaps, ...)
Financial employees:
  • The Front Office is composed of customer-facing employees
    • Traders buy or sell a product to a client
    • Sales communicate orders from bank's clients to appropriate trading desks
    • Market makers quote both a buy and a sell price in a financial instrument (profit on the bid-offer spread)
    • Arbitrageurs take advantage of market discrepancies
    • Quantitative analysts apply mathematical and statistical methods to financial and risk management problems
  • The Middle Office manages risk and maintains the information resources
  • The Back Office provides support, administrative and payment services
    • Human ressources
    • Office managers
    • Customer care
Two kinds of trading:
  • Exchange trading is done via exchangesi (organized markets)
  • Over-the-counter OTC trading is done between two parties without the supervision of an exchange
Three market levels:
  • Primary market: market for new issues or new financial claims (products issued to the public for the first time)
  • Secondary market: market for second hand products
